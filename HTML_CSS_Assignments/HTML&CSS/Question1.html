Interesting Facts about DR.APJ Abdul Kalam
Hardships
Jainulabudeen, Kalam’s father, was a boat owner and imam of a local mosque. His ancestors were wealthy but due to business failures, the family fortunes were lost and the former President was forced to do odd jobs. He used to sell newspapers to support his family.
Failures
Kalam missed an opportunity to become a fighter pilot for the Indian Air force. He was on the 9th spot on the list, and there were only 8 openings. The first 8 shortlisted candidates were recruited.Empowered Indian Nuclear Mission: Kalam also made significant contributions to the nuclear capabilities of India. The Pokhran 2 nuclear tests in 1998 were successful because of his technical support and knowledge.
Honours
He was honoured with doctorates from 40 universities.
Frisking at Airport
Kalam was frisked twice at JFK Airport in New York. He was checked for explosives by the airport staff. This incident was protested by India on the international level.
Science Day
Kalam’s visit to Switzerland on 26th May is commemorated as Science Day in the country. This declaration in his honour was made by the Swiss Government after his demise.
The VVS Connection
Kalam followed only one cricketer on his Twitter account – VVS Laxman. He followed just “38 people” on this social media account.
Youth Icon
Mr. Kalam was awarded the MTV Youth Icon award in 2003 and 2006.
People’s President
Once he used the Yahoo Answers platform to ask a question about terrorism. He asked, “What should we do to free our planet from terrorism?” He received more than 30,000 responses to his question.
Visionary
Kalam’s wish was to power the Rashtrapati Bhawan completely with solar power. However, this could not be implemented during the term of his presidency.
Died Doing what he Loved
On his last day, he was delivering a lecture at IIM, Shillong. He was delivering a speech to hundreds of students when he suffered a cardiac arrest that made him fall to the floor. He always had a passion for teaching and working hard. And at the time when death captured him, he was doing what he loved.
World Student’s Day
Abdul Kalam’s birthday i.e 15th October is observed as the World Student’s Day. This initiative was started by the UN in 2015. The motive behind this initiative is to promote diversity, multiculturalism, and innovativeness among students across the world.
